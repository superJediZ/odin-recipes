Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is one of the world's most beloved and enduring comfort foods, originating in Italy with roots that stretch back to ancient times. In its classic form, the dish is built on a simple yet brilliant architecture: wide, flat sheets of pasta layered with a rich variety of savory fillings. Over time, regional and cultural twists have introduced countless variations, ranging from the American-style version loaded with ricotta, mozzarella, and marinara sauce, to lighter, vegetable-forward renditions featuring spinach, mushrooms, and squash.

What makes lasagna truly special is its unique texture and the way it brings people together. As it bakes, the flavors meld into a harmonious blend of tender pasta, savory sauces, and a beautifully bubbled, golden-brown cheese crust. Because it requires a bit of time and patience to assemble, it is historically celebrated as a dish for Sunday dinners, holidays, and large family gatherings. It is the ultimate make-ahead meal, often tasting even better the next day as the layers set, making it a timeless staple of home-cooked comfort across the globe.

Ingredients

  • Parmasean Cheese
  • Lasanga Noodles
  • Crushed Tomato
  • Ground Meat
  • Aromatics
  • Veggies
  • Spices
  • Herbs

Steps

  1. Saute your diced aromatics and veggies in a large pot before adding the ground meat to brown. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, season with your herbs and spices, and let the sauce simmer to build flavor.
  2. Boil your lasagna noodles in salted water until they are just al dente so they don't get mushy. Alternatively, if you are using oven-ready noodles, briefly soak them in hot water to soften them up before layering.
  3. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish, then top it with a layer of noodles. Repeat the layers of noodles, meat sauce, and Parmesan cheese until the dish is full, finishing with a heavy blanket of cheese on top.
  4. Cover the dish with foil and bake at 190 degrees Celcius (375 degrees Farenheit) until bubbling, removing the foil for the last few minutes to brown the cheese. Let the lasagna rest for 20 minutes before slicing so the layers hold their shape perfectly.
